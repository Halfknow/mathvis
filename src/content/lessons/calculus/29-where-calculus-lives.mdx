You've traveled from slopes to areas, from derivatives to integrals, from sequences to series. Every idea connected back to the same core: **calculus is the mathematics of continuous change**. Before we see where it lives in the wild, step back and see the whole map at once. The derivative measures instantaneous change. The integral measures total accumulation. The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus connects them -- differentiation and integration are two sides of the same coin. Series approximate functions as infinite polynomials. Differential equations describe how systems evolve over time. Together, these ideas describe the entire physical world. Now let's see where calculus lives in the wild.

## Physics: the original application

Newton invented calculus to describe motion. His second law is a differential equation:

<MathBlock expression={"F = ma = m\\frac{d^2 x}{dt^2}"} />

From this one equation, plus gravity ($F = -mg$), you can derive projectile motion, planetary orbits, and harmonic oscillation. The derivative captures instantaneous velocity and acceleration; the integral recovers position from velocity.

## Machine learning: gradient descent

Every neural network is trained by gradient descent:

<MathBlock expression={"\\theta_{n+1} = \\theta_n - \\alpha \\nabla L(\\theta_n)"} />

The loss function $L$ is a surface in high-dimensional space. The gradient $\nabla L$ points uphill; you step downhill by $-\nabla L$. The learning rate $\alpha$ is the step size. This is literally multivariable calculus in action — partial derivatives, gradients, and optimization, all working together.

## Signal processing: Fourier series

Any periodic signal can be decomposed into sines and cosines:

<MathBlock expression={"f(t) = \\sum_{n=0}^{\\infty} a_n \\cos(n\\omega t) + b_n \\sin(n\\omega t)"} />

This is a Taylor-like idea but with trigonometric building blocks instead of polynomials. Audio compression, image processing, and wireless communication all rely on Fourier analysis — which is built on the convergence theory of infinite series.

## Engineering: differential equations everywhere

- **Heat equation**: $\partial u/\partial t = k \nabla^2 u$ — how temperature spreads through a material.
- **Wave equation**: $\partial^2 u/\partial t^2 = c^2 \nabla^2 u$ — how sound and light propagate.
- **Navier-Stokes**: $\rho(\partial \mathbf{v}/\partial t + \mathbf{v} \cdot \nabla \mathbf{v}) = -\nabla p + \mu \nabla^2 \mathbf{v}$ — how fluids flow (still unsolved in general).

## Economics: marginal analysis

The "marginal cost" is the derivative of the cost function. The "marginal revenue" is the derivative of the revenue function. Profit is maximized when marginal cost equals marginal revenue — $MC = MR$ is just $C'(x) = R'(x)$, a calculus optimization problem.

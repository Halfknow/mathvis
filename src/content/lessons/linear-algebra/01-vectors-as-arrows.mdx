# Vectors as Arrows

Before a vector is a list of numbers, it is an **arrow** — pointing from
somewhere, in some direction, with some length. Almost every theorem you'll
meet later has a geometric shadow you can see, if you train yourself to look
for it. This lesson is about training that eye.

## What an arrow knows

An arrow on the page captures two pieces of information at once: a
**direction** and a **magnitude**. That's it. It does not remember where
its tail starts — and this freedom is what gives vectors their power.

# Vectors as Arrows

Before a vector is a list of numbers, it is an **arrow** — pointing from
somewhere, in some direction, with some length. Almost every theorem you'll
meet later has a geometric shadow you can see, if you train yourself to look
for it. This lesson is about training that eye.

## What an arrow knows

An arrow on the page captures two pieces of information at once: a
**direction** and a **magnitude**. That's it. It does not remember where
its tail starts — and this freedom is what gives vectors their power.

phrase that observation in symbols only — *“the ordered pair $(2, 1)$

notice how unsatisfying the symbolic version is. The geometry is the point.

  A vector is the **equivalence class** of all arrows with the same
  direction and length. The coordinates we will introduce later are
  one specific representative — convenient, but never the whole story.
</KeyInsight>

## When coordinates enter the picture

Coordinates appear only when we *choose* a starting point — by convention,
the origin. With that choice fixed, the arrow's tip lands somewhere, and
those landing coordinates become the vector's components:

$$
\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}
$$

Notice the order of operations: **the geometry came first, the numbers
came second**. Almost every confusion in linear algebra traces back to
people inheriting the numbers and forgetting they were ever arrows.

<ExerciseBlock
  number={1}
  prompt={
    <>
      Two arrows are drawn on the plane. The first goes from <code>(0, 0)</code>
      {' '}to <code>(3, 1)</code>. The second goes from <code>(2, 4)</code>
      {' '}to <code>(5, 5)</code>. Do they represent the same vector?
    </>
  }
  hint={
    <>
      A vector is determined by how far it travels in <em>x</em> and how
      far it travels in <em>y</em>, regardless of where it starts. Compute
      $\Delta x$ and $\Delta y$ for each arrow.
    </>
  }
  answer={
    <>
      Yes. Both arrows have $\Delta x = 3$ and $\Delta y = 1$. Different
      tails, same vector.
    </>
  }
/>

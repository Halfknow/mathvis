We've computed areas and volumes. Now we measure **length** -- how long is a curve? -- and **surface area** -- how much paint to cover a surface of revolution? Arc length is the Pythagorean theorem distributed across infinitely many tiny right triangles. Each triangle has legs $dx$ and $dy$; the hypotenuse $\sqrt{dx^2 + dy^2}$ is the arc length element. The integral adds up all those tiny hypotenuses to get the total length.

## Arc length

A curve $y = f(x)$ from $x = a$ to $x = b$ can be approximated by many short line segments. Each segment has length $\sqrt{(\Delta x)^2 + (\Delta y)^2}$ by the Pythagorean theorem. Sum them and take the limit:

<MathBlock expression={"L = \\int_a^b \\sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2}\\, dx"} />

The formula is the integral version of the Pythagorean theorem: at each point, the infinitesimal arc length is $\sqrt{dx^2 + dy^2} = \sqrt{1 + (dy/dx)^2}\, dx$.

### Example: Length of $y = x^{3/2}$ from $x = 0$ to $x = 4$

<MathBlock expression={"f'(x) = \\frac{3}{2}x^{1/2}, \\quad [f'(x)]^2 = \\frac{9}{4}x"} />
<MathBlock expression={"L = \\int_0^4 \\sqrt{1 + \\frac{9}{4}x}\\, dx = \\frac{4}{9} \\cdot \\frac{2}{3}\\left(1 + \\frac{9}{4}x\\right)^{3/2}\\Big|_0^4 = \\frac{8}{27}(10^{3/2} - 1)"} />

## Surface area

When $y = f(x)$ from $x = a$ to $x = b$ is rotated around the x-axis, the surface area is:

<MathBlock expression={"S = 2\\pi \\int_a^b f(x) \\sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2}\\, dx"} />

This is the circumference ($2\pi r = 2\pi f(x)$) times the arc length element, integrated over the curve.

### Example: Surface area of a sphere

Rotate $y = \sqrt{R^2 - x^2}$ from $x = -R$ to $x = R$ around the x-axis.

<MathBlock expression={"f'(x) = \\frac{-x}{\\sqrt{R^2 - x^2}}, \\quad 1 + [f'(x)]^2 = \\frac{R^2}{R^2 - x^2}"} />
<MathBlock expression={"S = 2\\pi \\int_{-R}^{R} \\sqrt{R^2 - x^2} \\cdot \\frac{R}{\\sqrt{R^2 - x^2}}\\, dx = 2\\pi R \\int_{-R}^{R} dx = 4\\pi R^2"} />

The classic formula $S = 4\pi R^2$ emerges naturally from the integral.

<KeyInsight>
Arc length integrates the infinitesimal hypotenuse √(1 + f′²) along the curve. Surface area multiplies each arc length element by the circumference at that height. Both formulas extend the Pythagorean theorem to continuous curves.
</KeyInsight>

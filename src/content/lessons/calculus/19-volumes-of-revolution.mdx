Take a 2D curve and spin it around an axis. The curve sweeps out a surface, and the enclosed region becomes a 3D solid -- a **solid of revolution**. Two methods let you compute its volume: the disk/washer method and the shell method. Think of these as two camera angles on the same solid. Disk slices perpendicular to the axis, producing circular cross-sections; shell wraps vertical strips around the axis, producing cylindrical layers. Same volume, different integrals. Choose whichever angle gives the simpler calculation.

## The disk method (review)

Rotate $y = f(x)$ around the x-axis. Each cross-section perpendicular to the x-axis is a circular disk:

<MathBlock expression={"V = \\pi \\int_a^b [f(x)]^2\\, dx"} />

This works well when you can easily express the radius as a function of $x$.

## The shell method

Sometimes it's easier to think of the solid as a stack of **cylindrical shells** rather than disks. A vertical strip at position $x$ with height $f(x)$ and width $dx$, when rotated around the y-axis, becomes a thin cylindrical shell:

<MathBlock expression={"dV = 2\\pi \\cdot \\underbrace{x}_{\\text{radius}} \\cdot \\underbrace{f(x)}_{\\text{height}} \\cdot \\underbrace{dx}_{\\text{thickness}}"} />

Total volume:

<MathBlock expression={"V = 2\\pi \\int_a^b x \\cdot f(x)\\, dx"} />

## When to use which method?

- **Disk/washer**: when slices perpendicular to the axis of rotation give clean circular cross-sections.
- **Shell**: when vertical (or horizontal) strips give simpler integrands when wrapped around the axis.

The two methods give the same answer — they're just different ways of slicing the same solid.

## Example: rotate $y = x^2$ from $x = 0$ to $x = 2$ around the y-axis

**Shell method** (natural choice):

<MathBlock expression={"V = 2\\pi \\int_0^2 x \\cdot x^2\\, dx = 2\\pi \\int_0^2 x^3\\, dx = 2\\pi \\cdot \\frac{x^4}{4}\\Big|_0^2 = 2\\pi \\cdot 4 = 8\\pi"} />

**Disk method** (requires solving for x):

$y = x^2$ gives $x = \sqrt{y}$. The solid extends from $y = 0$ to $y = 4$.

<MathBlock expression={"V = \\pi \\int_0^4 [\\sqrt{y}]^2 - [0]^2\\, dy = \\pi \\int_0^4 y\\, dy = \\pi \\cdot \\frac{y^2}{2}\\Big|_0^4 = 8\\pi"} />

Same answer. The shell method was simpler because it didn't require solving for $x$.

<KeyInsight>
Disk method slices perpendicular to the axis; shell method wraps strips around the axis. They give the same volume but one is usually easier than the other. Choose based on which gives the simpler integral.
</KeyInsight>

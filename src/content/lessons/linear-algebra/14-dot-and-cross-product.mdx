# Dot Product and Cross Product

We've been transforming space. Now let's measure relationships *between* vectors. Two operations: the dot product asks "how aligned are these arrows?" and the cross product asks "what's perpendicular to both?"

## The dot product — measuring alignment

Take two arrows. The **dot product** is:

<MathBlock expression={'\\vec{a} \\cdot \\vec{b} = a_1 b_1 + a_2 b_2 = |\\vec{a}| \\, |\\vec{b}| \\cos\\theta'} />

where θ is the angle between them.

The geometric picture:
- **Positive** (θ < 90°): arrows point roughly the same way — they're somewhat aligned
- **Zero** (θ = 90°): arrows are perpendicular — completely unaligned
- **Negative** (θ > 90°): arrows point away from each other — anti-aligned

## Projection — the geometric meaning

The dot product equals |**a**| times the projection of **b** onto **a**. Think of shining a light perpendicular to **a**: the shadow **b** casts onto **a**'s line is its projection. The dot product scales that shadow by |**a**|.

This means: **a** · **b** answers "how much of **b** lies in the direction of **a**?" — weighted by **a**'s length.

## The cross product — building perpendicularity (3D)

In 3D, the **cross product** of two vectors produces a third vector perpendicular to both:

<MathBlock expression={'\\vec{a} \\times \\vec{b} = |\\vec{a}| \\, |\\vec{b}| \\sin\\theta \\, \\hat{n}'} />

The magnitude |**a** × **b**| equals the area of the parallelogram formed by **a** and **b**. The direction n̂ is perpendicular to both (given by the right-hand rule).

Key properties:
- **a** × **a** = **0** (parallel vectors span no area)
- **a** × **b** = -(**b** × **a**) (order flips the direction)
- |**a** × **b**| = 0 when **a** and **b** are parallel

<KeyInsight>
The dot product measures how much two arrows agree in direction — positive means aligned, zero means perpendicular, negative means opposed. The cross product builds a perpendicular arrow whose length equals the parallelogram area.
</KeyInsight>

## The connection to everything else

- **Dot product → projection**: decomposing vectors into components (used everywhere in physics and ML)
- **Dot product → angles**: cos θ = (**a** · **b**) / (|**a**||**b**|) — the universal angle formula
- **Cross product → area**: the area of the parallelogram (and triangle) formed by two vectors
- **Cross product → torque, angular momentum**: physics runs on the cross product
